Ahmed Mohammed

 

I am a Research scientist at Sintef Digital and an Adjunct associate professor position at NTNU. I did my Ph.D. at the department of computer science at the Norwegian University of Science and Technology, advised by Sule Yildirim Yayilgan, Marius Pedersen, and Øistein Hovde. Before that, I received a Master’s degree from Chonbuk National University (Jeonju, South Korea) in 2014 and a Bachelor’s degree from Addis Ababa University (Addis Ababa, Ethiopia) in 2009.

My research interests are broadly in Machine learning and computer vision, with emphasis on 3D vision and medical imaging for explainable and data-efficient learning. My current work focuses on deep learning for point cloud processing, underwater navigation, and capsule video endoscopy.

SFI Manufacturing

SFI Manufacturing’s vision is to show that sustainable and advanced production of goods in a high-cost country like Norway is possible, given the right products, the right technologies and the right people involved in the right way.

SIGHT

One of the biggest challenges in the construction industry is the gap between what is planned and what is actually being built. The current technology for detecting such construction defects is laser scanning, supplemented by manual sampling.

SmartFish

optimize resource efficiency, improve automatic data collection for fish stock assessment, provide evidence of compliance with fishery regulations and reduce the ecological impact of the industry.
